## AI Trends 2025 Research Notes

### Key Findings from "The 2025 AI Index Report"
1. **Improved Benchmarks**: AI performance on demanding benchmarks has significantly improved, including in generating high-quality videos and outperforming humans in specific programming tasks.
2. **Everyday AI Integration**: FDA approvals for AI-enabled medical devices surged to 223 in 2023. Autonomous rides by Waymo and Baidu's Apollo Go demonstrate AI's embedding in day-to-day life.
3. **Investment**: Private AI investment in the U.S. reached $109 billion in 2024. Generative AI attracted a global investment of $33.9 billion.
4. **Global Competition**: The U.S. leads in producing AI models, though China is rapidly closing the quality gap. China, meanwhile, leads in publications and patents.
5. **Responsible AI**: New frameworks such as HELM Safety and AIR-Bench have been introduced, but industry-wide adoption remains slow.
6. **Optimism Levels**: Optimism towards AI varies globally, with nations like China showing a majority seeing benefits, while sentiment is lower in places such as the U.S. 
7. **Affordability and Efficiency**: GPT-3.5-like models' inference costs dropped drastically (280-fold) while hardware costs and energy consumption also decreased significantly.
8. **Government Role**: Countries are investing more in AI regulatory and development efforts, with initiatives from Canada, China, France, and Saudi Arabia.

### Insights from "The State of AI: Global Survey 2025" (McKinsey Analysis)
1. **Adoption Stages**: While AI adoption has reached 88% in at least one function across organizations, many are still experimenting and piloting.
2. **AI Agents**: Roughly 62% of surveyed organizations are experimenting with AI agents. Scalability is limited primarily to IT and knowledge management.
3. **Benefits Reported**: AI is credited for fostering innovation, enhancing customer satisfaction, and yielding higher competitiveness. Companies focusing on growth and innovation see the most productivity gains.
4. **Workflow Transformation**: Leading organizations redesign workflows to maximize AI. They are three times more likely to achieve transformative change.
5. **Senior Leadership Role**: High performers indicate stronger commitments and active engagement from senior leadership.
6. **Scaling Challenges**: 36% of organizations spending 20% or more of digital budgets on AI report quicker scaling achievements.
